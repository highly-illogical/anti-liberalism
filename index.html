Anti-Liberalism
A funny thing about modern anti-liberal movements is that though they are almost always at loggerheads with each other, they also bear strong similarities to each other.
These similarities remain obscured by the thick fog of stereotyping through which each of them perceives all the rest. But they are there. Take, for instance
Communists
Hindutva fascists
Islamists
They believe that liberal democracy is irredeemably corrupt and oppressive, and in its place
want to establish a utopia called
socialism

sharia law

a society of, by and for
the proletariat
Hindus
devout Muslims
who are the only group on earth that are truly capable of good, and in whom all goodness resides.
All other groups recognize and act in their own interest - only this group is too naive to do so, and is manipulated into believing this weakness to be virtue. If the state and its institutions are to be a force for good, they must be based upon the ideology that advocates for the interests of this group. Otherwise, they are oppressive, evil and worse than useless, for they enable the depraved groups of the world to dominate, subjugate and exploit the noble one.

the dictatorship of the proletariat

Christian, Jewish and democratic assaults on Muslims
While there may often be more than a grain of truth to this, an exclusive focus on this narrative allows them to paint all of social and political life as a conflict between the good victim group and evil oppressor groups.

All concepts of value have to be reinterpreted in terms of the basic conflict between the good group and the evil groups. "Freedom", especially, can only be the freedom to do what the ideology mandates - otherwise it cannot be considered a value at all.

While many of its criticisms of liberalism are valid, its real issue seems to be that liberalism has no interest in creating what it deems a perfect society. It is
against neoliberalism because

This template is shared across many movements from widely varying political, cultural and historical contexts. And it isn't hard to see why, given how effective it is.
